Career Milestones that Contributed to Her Net Worth

Diahann Carroll’s career is filled with remarkable milestones that significantly contributed to her net worth.

She made history as the first African-American woman to win a Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical for her role in No Strings in 1962. This groundbreaking achievement opened many doors for her in the entertainment industry.

Her success continued with her iconic role in the television series Julia (1968-1971), which earned her a Golden Globe Award.

This series was revolutionary as it was the first on American television to star a black woman in a non-stereotypical role.

In film, Carroll’s performance in Claudine (1974) earned her an Academy Award nomination for Best Actress. These major awards and recognitions not only boosted her fame but also her financial standing.

FAQs about Diahann Carroll

Who was Diahann Carroll?

Diahann Carroll was an iconic actress and singer known for breaking barriers in the entertainment industry. She was the first African American woman to star in her own TV series in a non-stereotypical role with the show Julia in 1968.

What is her most famous role?

Carroll is best known for her role as Dominique Deveraux in the popular 1980s TV series Dynasty. She also garnered acclaim for her groundbreaking role in Julia and her performances in Claudine and on Broadway.

How did she start her career?

Carroll’s career began in the 1950s after winning a talent show on the TV program Chance of a Lifetime. This led to nightclub engagements and roles in major films like Carmen Jones and Porgy and Bess.

What awards did Diahann Carroll win?

Carroll won a Tony Award for Best Actress in a Musical for No Strings in 1962, a Golden Globe for her role in Julia, and she was nominated for an Academy Award for Best Actress for Claudine in 1974.

What impact did she have on television?

Carroll’s role in Julia was groundbreaking as it portrayed an African American woman in a professional role rather than a stereotypical domestic worker, paving the way for more diverse and substantial roles for African Americans on TV.

Did she write any books?

Yes, she authored two memoirs: Diahann! published in 1986 and The Legs Are the Last to Go: Aging, Acting, Marrying, and Other Things I Learned the Hard Way in 2008, where she discussed her career and personal life.

What were some of Diahann Carroll’s notable film roles?

Apart from Carmen Jones and Porgy and Bess, Carroll starred in Paris Blues (1961), Claudine (1974), and The Five Heartbeats (1991). Her role in Claudine earned her an Oscar nomination.

What was her personal life like?

Carroll was married four times and had one daughter, Suzanne Kay. She was diagnosed with breast cancer in 1997 and became an advocate for cancer awareness until her death in 2019.

How did Diahann Carroll die?

She passed away on October 4, 2019, at her home in West Hollywood, California, from complications related to breast cancer at the age of 84.
